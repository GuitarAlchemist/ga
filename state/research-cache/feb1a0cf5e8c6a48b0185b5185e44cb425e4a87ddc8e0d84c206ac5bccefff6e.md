# Research Cache: Spectral Music Intelligence Track Foundations

## JEPA/SSL Architectures for Music (2024)

**Stem-JEPA (ISMIR 2024, Sony CSL Paris)**
- Authors: Alain Riou, Stefan Lattner, Gaëtan Hadjeres, Michael Anslow, Geoffroy Peeters
- Joint-Embedding Predictive Architecture trained on multi-track dataset
- Consists of encoder + predictor, jointly trained to predict stem compatibility
- Recall @1: 33.0% on retrieval tasks; competitive on downstream tagging tasks
- Demonstrates JEPA viability for audio music analysis

**Audio-JEPA**
- Adapts JEPA framework to audio domain using mel-spectrogram patches
- Context encoder + lightweight predictor + momentum-updated target encoder
- Performance comparable to wav2vec 2.0 and data2vec using <1/5 of their training data
- Proves JEPA scalability in audio with reduced data requirements

**MERT**
- Self-supervised learning applied to acoustic music understanding
- Represents gap-filling in SSL application to music audio

## Symbolic Chord Prediction Corpora (2024)

**McGill Billboard Dataset**
- 742 unique songs from American Billboard charts (1958-1991)
- Pop and rock music with comprehensive expert chord annotations
- Widely used baseline in MIR tasks
- Multiple format availability (humdrum, kern, MIDI)

**Hooktheory/TheoryTab Dataset**
- 18,843 annotated music sections
- Human-transcribed melodies + chord progressions
- Chord database: 75,000+ song analyses (largest public theory database)
- API available for chord probability queries
- I chord: 18.9% frequency; IV: 17.2% in Western pop corpus

**CoCoPops (Combined Corpus of Popular Music)**
- Billboard subcorpus: McGill Billboard converted to humdrum + 214 new melodic transcriptions
- Rolling Stone subcorpus: 200 songs with melody/harmony in humdrum format
- Cross-format compatibility (humdrum, MIDI, musicXML)

**CHORDONOMICON (2024)**
- 666,000 songs with chord progressions
- Annotated with structural parts (verse/chorus/bridge), genre, release date
- User-generated progressions from multiple sources
- Largest symbolic chord progression dataset to date
- Supports deep learning for chord generation/analysis

**Lakh MIDI Dataset**
- 176,581 unique MIDI files
- 45,129 aligned to Million Song Dataset
- ~9,000+ hours multi-instrumental, multi-genre music
- Widest genre coverage (pop, classical, jazz)

**Weimar Jazz Database / Jazzomat**
- 456 high-quality solo transcriptions from 343 tracks (197 records)
- Extensive metadata: style, genre, rhythm feel, tonality, key, tempo class
- Solos 1925-2009 (Armstrong, Potter, Byas, etc.)
- Manually annotated by musicology students with SonicVisualiser

**KernScores (CCARH, Stanford)**
- Part of broader **kern collection: 100,000+ files
- Multiple representations: score image, kern encoding, MIDI
- Pioneer in symbolic notation for computational musicology
- 221+ pieces used in research fine-tuning studies

## Music Theory: Fourier Phases & Chord-Scale Compatibility

**Ian Quinn's DFT on Pitch-Class Sets**
- Discrete Fourier Transform magnitudes → harmonic qualities
- "General Equal-Tempered Harmony" (Perspectives of New Music, 2006)
- Spectral information correlates with harmonic consonance
- Foundation for 20th-century harmony theory via DFT

**Emmanuel Amiot, "Music Through Fourier Space" (Springer, 2016)**
- Comprehensive treatment of DFT applied to pcsets
- Phases of Fourier components define harmonic geometry
- Phase-based harmony space: resembles Tonnetz but continuous
- 3rd & 5th component phases: chord classification in 2D space
- Validates magnitude + phase approaches for harmony analysis

**Fourier Phase Space Applications**
- Schubert harmonic language analysis via DFT phases
- Continuous harmonic geometry (vs discrete Tonnetz)
- Common-tone basis topology preservation in phase space
- Wider domain of harmonic objects than magnitude alone

**Tymoczko: Maximally Even Sets & Voice-Leading**
- Nearly even chords: diatonic (7-in-12), pentatonic (5-in-12), whole-tone (6-in-12), diminished (8-in-12)
- Maximally even pitch distribution → minimal voice-leading distance
- Optimal transposition properties for ME chords
- Set-class space geometry for harmonic analysis
- Parsimonious voice-leading (common-tone maximization)

## Existing Products & Competitive Differentiation

**Hooktheory Trends**
- Database: 75,000+ song analyses
- API: chord probability, next-chord prediction, progression search
- Statistical corpus-driven rather than generative
- Web + mobile; no spectral/fretboard features

**Chordify**
- Automatic chord recognition from audio (YouTube, Spotify, mic input)
- 4.5M pre-chordified user library
- Real-time chord display + animated fretboard (guitar/ukulele/piano)
- Premium: transpose, capo, loop, slow, download MIDI/PDF
- No spectral analysis; audio→symbolic only

**iReal Pro**
- Jazz fakebook: 1000s of chord charts (free from forums)
- Realistic backing tracks (50+ styles: jazz, Latin, pop, bluegrass, rock, reggae)
- Live chord/diagram display (piano, guitar, ukulele, notation)
- Chart editing + custom track generation
- No spectral; chord-centered, performance-focused

**Songsterr**
- 1M+ guitar/bass/drum tabs with rhythm
- Interactive playback + animated fretboard
- Synchronized original audio (muted parts available)
- Speed control, loop, pitch shift, tuner, offline mode
- Tablature visualization, not spectral or chord-specific

## Legal/Licensing Landscape

**Ultimate Guitar Licensing**
- 2010: Harry Fox Agency agreement (lyrics, tabs, download/print)
- Sony/ATV, Alfred Music confirm proper licensing
- MPA legal action (2005+): shutdowns for unlicensed sites
- Song blocking: owners can restrict display/use
- Scraping: legally risky without licensing agreement

**Tab/Chord Licensing Risk**
- Guitar tab copyright: subject of ongoing disputes
- Fair use uncertain for derivative works (annotations, transcriptions)
- Database scraping: legal if licensed, risky if not
- Chord progressions themselves: less protected than melodies

## Research Opportunity Gaps

1. **No published JEPA for symbolic chord prediction** (only audio JEPA/Stem-JEPA)
2. **Spectral phase-matching for chord-scale compatibility**: validated by theory (Quinn, Amiot, Tymoczko) but not yet automated in production tools
3. **Arpeggio path cost minimization**: not addressed by Hooktheory, Chordify, iReal Pro, or Songsterr
4. **Fretboard 3D visualization + spectral analysis**: unique to proposed GA spectral track
5. **Symbolic music SSL/JEPA**: no published models, only audio-domain SSL
