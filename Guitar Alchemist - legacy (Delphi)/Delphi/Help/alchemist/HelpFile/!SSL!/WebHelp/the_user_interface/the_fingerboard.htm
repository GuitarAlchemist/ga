The fingerboard

 

The fingerboard can be userd to visualize scales and chords.

At any time, you can play a fingerboard position by clicking on the mouse.

You can customize the aspect of the fingerboard in the fingerboard options page.

 

 

Example of scale displayed on the fingerboard


When a scale is displayed on the fingerboard, the pattern positions (positions reached by the fingers without moving the hand) are shown in color, while the other positions are optionally displayed in gray. You can hide these non-pattern positions and change the transparency level in the options.

Each color corresponds to one of the 12 possible intervals, and the root of the scale is colored in black. The numbers inside the circle identifies the intervals. Positions with lines beside are called the character tones.

 

 

Example of chord displayed on the fingerboard

 

When a chord is displayed on the fingerboard, the chord positions are displayed in black, while the other scale positions are optionally displayed in gray. The number inside the circle identifies the intervals.

You can hide these non-pattern positions and change the transparency level in the options.

The X represent muted positions.
