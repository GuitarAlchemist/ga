The matching chords panel

 

This panel can be hidden by click on the little button on the extreme right of the panel header.

 

The panel headers displays the selected scale.

Below the header, there's a list of all the chord patterns that match this scale. The patterns in bold are those constructed by stacking the intervals of a 3rd above the root note, which is the most common way of constructing the chords from a scale. On the right of the chord template name, a symbol indicates the function of the chords which be major, minor, dominant or special. On the right of the symbol is displayed the formula of the template (i.e. All the interval qualities that the chord template contains). Just click on a chord template to play it. A few moments after the template is selected, all the chord voicing for this template will be computed and listed on the chord dictionary. You can also double click on the selected template to force the chord dictionary to refresh the list right away.
