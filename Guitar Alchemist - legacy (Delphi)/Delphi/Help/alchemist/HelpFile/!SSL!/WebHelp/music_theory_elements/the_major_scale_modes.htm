The major scale modes

 

Let's first explain what a mode is. A mode is an inverted scale, where one of the notes from the scale functions as the starting point (tonic or root) and is the focal point of the scale.

In the C major scale, this note is C. When C is the root or tonic tone, we call it a C major scale.

Each mode of the major scale has a greek name.
