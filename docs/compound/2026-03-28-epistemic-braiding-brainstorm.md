## Breakthroughs

### 1. Epistemic Braiding (not Meta-Cognition)

"Meta" implies a stack (object -> meta -> meta-meta). The reality is a **braid**: five strands running in parallel, coupling at specific points.

| Strand | Domain | Direction | Halting Condition |
|--------|--------|-----------|-------------------|
| Belief | What I know | Convergent | Contradictory Ground |
| Strategy | How I learn | Convergent | Diminishing returns |
| Explanation | How I teach | Divergent | Learner coverage threshold |
| Affect | How I feel about knowing | Oscillating | Discomfort resolution |
| Perturbation | How introspection changes me | Observational | Logged, never controlled |

Six coupling points:
- Belief -> Explanation ("I teach what I know")
- Explanation -> Belief ("Teaching reveals what I don't know")
- Affect -> Strategy ("Anxiety triggers strategy switching")
- Strategy -> Belief ("The strategy chosen determines what I learn")
- Perturbation -> Affect ("Noticing I changed makes me uneasy")
- Belief -> Affect ("Viscous beliefs trigger discomfort")

### 2. Contradictory Ground Theorem

In tetravalent logic, Contradictory is the only truth state that is a fixed point under the meta-operation M(B). All other states can have different meta-assessments, but M(C) = C always.

**Governance rule**: When meta-reflection reaches state C at any depth, stop reflecting. Log the grounding event. Act (PDCA, consult, gather evidence). Schedule re-examination after action resolves.

This is not an arbitrary depth cap -- it's a principled halting condition derived from the logic itself.

### 3. Epistemic Viscosity

The resistance of a belief to change under new evidence. Distinct from confidence (how sure) and durability (how long it lasts). Viscosity is the third derivative: how responsive is the rate of change to new evidence?

High-viscosity beliefs are the most dangerous in governance -- unfalsifiable not because they're true, but because they've become load-bearing for other beliefs.

### 4. Teaching-as-Validation Gate

Before any high-stakes belief is committed to durable store, Demerzel must generate a Streeling lesson plan for it. If she cannot construct a coherent explanation, the belief is insufficiently grounded.

This couples the convergent arm (belief compression) with the divergent arm (explanation expansion). You cannot compress until you've tried to expand.
